I'm currently gearing up for the [dSAT](https://satsuite.collegeboard.org/sat), and this space aims to capture all the knowledge I need to catch up. It's been a while since I did any kind of maths, so I'll need to work towards remembering even basic concepts...

From the initial diagnostic test, I'll need to have a look again at these topics:

- Quadratic equations
	- Understanding the different forms, and how to convert between them (i.e. standard, factored, and vertex)
	- Completing the square, which is something I've always kinda struggled with
	- Components of a quadratic equation — that is, given $ax^2 + bx + c$, what do $a$, $b$, and $c$ signify?
- $n$-degree polynomials
- Basic arithmetic
	- Long division of large numbers (i.e. two numbers greater than 10)
	- Multiplication of large numbers (i.e. two numbers greater than 10)
	- Multiplication of decimals without a calculator
	- Simplifying "common" denominators (e.g. $\frac {a + b} {c - d} - \frac {a + b} {d - c} = \frac {a + b} {c - d} - \frac {a + b} {-1 (c - d)}$)
- Geometric formulae
	- Circles — formulae and properties (e.g. sectors)
- Symbols
	- $||$
